# How to Appeal an Alberta Benefit Decision
### Step-by-Step Guide in Plain Language
### Written for clients who are doing this for the first time
*SCSS AB Advocate — Client Appeal Guide*

---

## First — Take a Breath. You Can Do This.

Getting a letter saying your benefits are cut or denied is scary and upsetting.
But you have the right to fight it — and many appeals are won.

This guide walks you through every step.

---

## STEP 1 — Read the Decision Letter Carefully

When you get a letter from Alberta Works or AISH, look for:

- [ ] The date on the letter (this starts your 30-day clock)
- [ ] What exactly they decided
- [ ] Why they say they made this decision
- [ ] Whether there is a section about your right to appeal

**Write down the date you received the letter.**
If you are not sure when it arrived, use today's date — but act fast.

---

## STEP 2 — Do NOT Miss the 30-Day Deadline

You have **30 calendar days** from the date of the decision to file your appeal.

Count carefully:
- Day 1 = the day after the decision date on the letter
- Day 30 = your last day to file

**If you are close to the deadline — do this TODAY.**
You can file your appeal first and add details later.

**What if I missed the deadline?**
You can still ask for an extension — explain why you were late.
Common accepted reasons: health crisis, not receiving the letter, domestic emergency.
Do not assume it is too late without asking.

---

## STEP 3 — Write Down Your Side of the Story

Before you talk to anyone, write down:

1. What happened in your own words
2. Why you think the decision is wrong
3. Any important facts they may not know about you
4. Any documents you have (doctor notes, receipts, letters, emails)

You do not need to use legal language. Just tell the truth clearly.

---

## STEP 4 — File Your Notice of Appeal

**Option A — Do it online:**
Go to: `alberta.ca` → search "appeal income support decision"
Fill out the online form.

**Option B — Do it by phone:**
Call the Appeals Secretariat: **1-780-644-3456**
Tell them: *"I want to file a Notice of Appeal."*

**Option C — Do it in person:**
Go to your nearest Alberta Works office.
Ask for a Notice of Appeal form.

**What you will need to tell them:**
- Your name and contact information
- What decision you are appealing
- The date of the decision
- A brief reason why you are appealing (you can just say "I disagree with the decision")

---

## STEP 5 — Ask for Your File (FOIP Request)

As soon as you file your appeal, ask your worker or caseworker:
*"I would like a copy of my file and all documents related to this decision."*

This is your right under FOIP. It is free.

Your file will show you:
- What your caseworker wrote about you
- What information they used to make the decision
- Whether there are any errors you can point to

---

## STEP 6 — Wait for Your Hearing Date

After you file, the Appeals Secretariat will contact you with:
- The date and time of your hearing
- Information about whether it will be written or oral

**You can ask for an oral hearing** (speaking in person or by phone).
Oral hearings are usually better — you can explain your situation directly.

**You can bring someone with you** — a friend, family member, or advocate.

---

## STEP 7 — Prepare for Your Hearing

Before your hearing, gather:

**Documents that help your case:**
- Doctor's notes or medical records (if health is involved)
- Letters from employers, landlords, or others
- Any emails or letters from your caseworker
- Receipts or bank statements if amounts are disputed
- Any policy documents that support your position (your worker can help with this)

**Write out what you want to say:**
- Start with: "I am appealing because..."
- Explain your situation
- Point to any documents that support you
- Say what you want the panel to do

**Practice saying it out loud** — even just once.

---

## STEP 8 — At Your Hearing

**You will be asked to present your case.**

Tips:
- Speak clearly and take your time
- Refer to your documents
- Say if you don't understand a question — it is okay to ask for clarification
- Stick to facts — explain what happened
- Be respectful even if you are frustrated

**The panel must be neutral.** They are not on Alberta Works' side.
They are there to decide if the decision was correct based on the rules.

---

## STEP 9 — After the Hearing

The panel will give you a decision — usually within a few weeks.

**If you win:**
- Alberta Works must follow the panel's decision
- Make sure your benefits are reinstated or corrected

**If you lose:**
- Ask your worker: "What are my options from here?"
- You may be able to apply for a judicial review
- You can contact the Alberta Ombudsman if the process was unfair
- You can re-apply if your circumstances change

---

## QUICK REFERENCE — KEY WORDS

| Word | What It Means |
|------|--------------|
| **Appeal** | Formally disagreeing with a government decision |
| **Notice of Appeal** | The form you fill out to start an appeal |
| **Appeals Secretariat** | The office that handles benefit appeals in Alberta |
| **CAP** | Citizens Appeal Panel — the group that hears your appeal |
| **ETW** | Expected to Work — a benefit category |
| **FOIP** | Your right to see your own government file |
| **Disclosure** | Documents Alberta Works must share with you before your hearing |
| **Oral Hearing** | Hearing where you speak instead of just submitting papers |
| **Judicial Review** | Asking a court to check if the CAP decision was legal |

---

## YOU DO NOT HAVE TO DO THIS ALONE

Your worker or advocate can:
- Help you file the appeal
- Request your file
- Prepare your hearing documents
- Come with you to the hearing
- Use the SCSS AB Advocate tool to research your case

**Ask for help. That is what we are here for.**
