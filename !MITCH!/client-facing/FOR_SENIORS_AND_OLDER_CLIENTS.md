Many seniors and older Albertans are not getting all the help they are entitled to.

The rules are complicated.
The letters from the government are hard to read.
The process can feel overwhelming.

**But there are people who can help you — and this office is one of them.**

---

# WHAT BENEFITS MIGHT YOU BE ENTITLED TO?

---

## Alberta Seniors Benefit

**Monthly money for lower-income Alberta seniors.**

You may qualify if you:
- Are 65 years of age or older
- Live in Alberta
- Have a lower income

Even if you are already getting some help, you might be entitled to more.
Ask your worker: *"Am I getting the full Alberta Seniors Benefit I qualify for?"*

---

## AISH — Assured Income for the Severely Handicapped

**Monthly support for people with serious long-term disabilities.**

You may qualify if you:
- Have a physical or mental condition that severely limits your ability to work
- Are between 18 and 65 years old
- Have low income and assets

AISH pays significantly more than regular Income Support.
**If you have a disability and are not on AISH — please ask your worker about it.**

---

## Income Support (Alberta Works)

**Help with basic living costs for people who need it.**

This can help pay for:
- Food and household basics
- Rent or housing costs
- Some medical costs

---

## SCSS — Seniors and Community Support Services

**Programs to help seniors stay healthy and independent.**

This includes:
- Home care
- Designated supportive living
- Long-term care
- Mobility and daily living support

# IF YOU GOT A BAD LETTER FROM THE GOVERNMENT

---

## Step 1 — Do Not Throw It Away

Keep every letter the government sends you.
Even if it is confusing or upsetting — keep it.
Bring it to your worker.

---

## Step 2 — Look at the Date

There is usually a deadline to respond or appeal.
**It is usually 30 days from the date on the letter.**

If you are not sure how much time you have — ask your worker TODAY.

---

## Step 3 — You Have the Right to Appeal

**If the government reduces or ends your benefits, you can fight it.**

Many seniors do not know this.
Many seniors win their appeals.

You do not need a lawyer to appeal.
Your worker can help you.

---

## Step 4 — Ask Your Worker for Help

Your worker can:
- Read the letter with you
- Explain what it means in plain language
- Find out if the government made a mistake
- Help you appeal if needed
- Go with you to a hearing

**You do not have to understand everything yourself. That is what your worker is for.**

---

# QUESTIONS YOU CAN ASK YOUR WORKER

It is always okay to ask questions. Here are some good ones:

- *"Am I getting all the benefits I am entitled to?"*
- *"Can you explain this letter to me in plain language?"*
- *"How long do I have to appeal this decision?"*
- *"What happens if I do not appeal?"*
- *"Can you come with me to the hearing?"*
- *"Is there any money available to help me right now?"*

# A NOTE ABOUT THE COMPUTER TOOL WE USE

When you meet with your worker, they may use a computer program to help with your case.

**This program is called the SCSS AB Advocate.**

Here is what you need to know:

- It is **not** a government website
- It is **on your side**
- It helps your worker find information faster
- Your worker still makes all decisions — the computer is just a helper
- Your personal information (like your SIN) should NOT be typed into it
- What you share with your worker stays with your worker
