# Your Rights — Alberta Benefit Programs
### Plain Language Guide for Clients
*SCSS AB Advocate — Client Rights Sheet*

---

## YOU HAVE RIGHTS. HERE THEY ARE.

Many people do not know their rights when dealing with Alberta Works,
AISH, or SCSS. This sheet explains them in plain language.

---

## THE RIGHT TO APPEAL

**If the government makes a decision you disagree with — you can fight it.**

This includes decisions about:
- Being denied benefits
- Having your benefits cut or reduced
- Being placed in a category you disagree with (like ETW)
- Being told you do not qualify for a program

**You have 30 days from the date of the decision letter to file an appeal.**
Do NOT wait. If you miss the deadline, you may lose your right to appeal.

**How to start an appeal:**
Contact your worker or the Appeals Secretariat directly:
- Phone: 1-780-644-3456
- Online: alberta.ca → search "appeal income support decision"

---

## THE RIGHT TO SEE YOUR OWN FILE

**You have the right to see everything the government has about you.**

This is called a **FOIP Request** (Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy).

Ask for your file if:
- You want to know what your caseworker wrote about you
- You were denied and want to know why
- You think there are errors in your file

**How to request your file:**
Ask your worker to help you file a FOIP request, or contact:
- Alberta FOIP Coordinator: 1-780-427-5848
- It is FREE to request your own personal information

---

## THE RIGHT TO A HEARING

**You can ask for a real hearing in front of a panel of people.**

This is called a **Citizens Appeal Panel (CAP) Hearing**.

At the hearing:
- You can bring someone with you (a friend, family member, or advocate)
- You can present documents and evidence
- You can speak for yourself
- The panel must give you a fair chance to be heard

**You can choose:**
- Written hearing (submit documents only)
- Oral hearing (speak in person or by phone) — this is usually better

---

## THE RIGHT TO BE TREATED WITH DIGNITY

**Caseworkers must treat you with respect.**

If a caseworker:
- Speaks to you in a demeaning or disrespectful way
- Refuses to give you information you're entitled to
- Makes decisions based on incorrect information
- Does not follow the rules properly

...you can complain. Ask your worker about filing a complaint.
You can also contact the **Alberta Ombudsman**: 1-888-455-2756

---

## THE RIGHT TO ACCURATE INFORMATION

**The government must apply the rules correctly.**

Caseworkers sometimes make mistakes. They may:
- Apply the wrong rate
- Use an outdated policy
- Misunderstand your situation

**You do not have to accept a wrong decision.**
Ask your worker to check the policy and compare it to what you were told.

---

## THE RIGHT TO EMERGENCY ASSISTANCE

**If you are in a crisis, you may qualify for emergency funds immediately.**

Alberta Works has emergency financial assistance for:
- Eviction / housing emergency
- No food
- Medical emergency costs
- Utilities being cut off

**Do not wait for a regular appointment if you are in crisis.**
Go in person or call your local Alberta Works office and say:
*"I am in an emergency situation and need immediate help."*

---

## THE RIGHT TO FAIR ETW ASSESSMENT

**If you are placed in the ETW (Expected to Work) category,
the government must assess you fairly.**

You may be exempt from ETW if you have:
- A physical or mental health condition
- A disability
- Caregiving responsibilities (young children, ill family member)
- Barriers to employment (housing instability, trauma, addiction recovery)

**If you believe ETW does not fit your situation — say so and ask for a review.**
You have the right to have your circumstances properly considered.

---

## THE RIGHT TO KEEP APPEALING

**If you lose at the CAP hearing, you are not necessarily done.**

You may be able to:
- Apply for a judicial review (ask a court to review the decision)
- Contact the Alberta Ombudsman if the process was unfair
- Re-apply if your circumstances change

**Always ask: "What are my options from here?"**
