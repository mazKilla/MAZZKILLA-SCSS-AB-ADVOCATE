# What Is This Tool?
### A Plain-Language Guide for Alberta Benefit Clients
*SCSS AB Advocate — Client Information Sheet*

---

## This Tool Is Here to Help You

If you are getting help from this office or this worker today, they may use a computer program called the **SCSS AB Advocate** to help with your case.

**This tool is a helper — it is NOT a government website.**
It is NOT run by Alberta Works, AISH, or any government office.
It is on the side of **YOU** — the client.

---

## What Can This Tool Do for Me?

This tool knows a lot about Alberta benefit programs and your rights.

It can help your worker:

**Find out what benefits you might be entitled to**
There are many programs in Alberta. This tool helps make sure nothing is missed.

**Explain why a decision against you might be wrong**
If the government denied your benefits or cut them, this tool can help find the reasons to fight back.

**Help prepare your appeal**
If you want to appeal a decision, this tool can help write the words you need.

**Explain your rights in plain language**
You have more rights than you might think. This tool knows them.

**Look up current government policy**
Rules change. This tool checks current Alberta policy so your worker has the latest information.

---

## What Programs Does This Tool Know About?

- **Alberta Works / Income Support** — monthly money for people who need it
- **ETW (Expected to Work)** — a category that affects how much support you get
- **ETWB (Expected to Work with Barriers)** — if you have health or other barriers
- **AISH (Assured Income for the Severely Handicapped)** — for people with serious disabilities
- **SCSS (Seniors and Community Support Services)** — programs for seniors
- **CAP (Citizens Appeal Panel)** — where you go when you disagree with a decision
- **FOIP** — your right to see your own government file

---

## Is My Information Safe?

**Yes — with a few important notes:**

- Your worker should NOT type your full legal name, Social Insurance Number (SIN), or file number into this tool
- They should use your initials or a case code
- The tool is run on a private computer — it is NOT a government system
- It does NOT report anything to Alberta Works or any government office
- Your conversations are stored on your worker's private database — not shared publicly

---

## This Tool Is a Helper — Not a Lawyer

This tool gives information and helps with research and writing.
It is **not** a lawyer and cannot give legal advice.
It **cannot** file anything on your behalf.
Your worker still reviews everything before it is used.

**Think of it like a very knowledgeable research assistant — not a replacement for your advocate.**

---

## If You Have Questions

Ask your worker. They can show you what the tool said and explain it.
You are always allowed to ask what information was used and why.
